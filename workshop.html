What it is

The Where's the Gap? Workshop is a live, facilitated session built on one question: where is the distance between what you actually mean and what people are hearing?

It's not a lecture. It's not a webinar. It's not a course you watch later. It's a working session — Sharéa in the room, you doing the thinking, both of you finding the gap together.

The workshop builds on the same framework as the free self-audit — five dimensions, five places the gap tends to live. The difference is what happens when you're not working through it alone.

What happens in the room

1
You come in

Participants complete the self-audit before the session so the work can start from a known place, not from scratch.

2
We find it

The session moves through your specific dimensions: where you're clear, where you're not, and what's creating the distance.

3
You leave with something

Not a workbook to file away. A specific named gap, a direction for closing it, and a clear next step.

Who this is for

This session is for someone who:

  • Has existing language about their work that isn't quite landing
  • Has taken the self-audit and wants to go deeper with support
  • Has been told their communication "needs work" by rooms that set the standard before they arrived
  • Is preparing something specific — a pitch, a relaunch, a shift in positioning — and wants a thinking partner who won't need a three-hour briefing to understand the problem

What you're getting in the room

Sharéa Harris has been facilitating writing and communications workshops for over a decade — at the United States Naval Academy, at the University of Baltimore, at UMBC, and through PRSA's Strategic Communications certificate program. She has built workshops for engineers learning to communicate technical work, for writing center tutors learning to hold antiracist space, for graduate students learning to write for medical and research audiences.

What she brings into the Where's the Gap? Workshop isn't a framework she found. It's a framework she built — from the classroom, the writing center, the briefing room, and the sprint. She knows where the gap lives because she's been helping people find it for years.
